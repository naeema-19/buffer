About Us

BUFF3R-CTF MACE EDITION is a CTF event organized by students of GDSC MACE in association with BUFF3R EVENTS

About BUFF3R-CTF

  • It is a Jeopardy style CTF
  • First round will be 24-hour online challenge
  • Second round will be 12-hour offline challenge
  • The top participants of round 1 will get selected to the round 2 and compete for the throne
  • There will be No third prize, No Second prize, only one winner, Only one king....
  • Participation certificates will be provided to every contestant and achievement certificates will be provided to finalists.
  • Activity points will be provided as proposed by the university

About GDSC MACE

GDSC MACE, is the college chapter of the Google Developer Student Club of Mar Athanasius College of Engineering. Formed in the year 2020, it has since become an active medium to put forward creative initiatives to enhance the potential abilities in students.

Rules for BUFF3R-CTF

  • Only students enrolled to part time/full time courses from any university/institution are allowed to participate.
  • No cooperation between teams with independent accounts. Sharing of keys or providing revealing hints to other teams is strictly prohibited.
  • No attacking the competition infrastructure. If bugs or vulns are found, please alert the competition organisers immediately.
  • Absolutely no sabotaging of other competing teams, or in any way hindering their independent progress.
  • No brute forcing of challenge flags against the scoring site. (Brute forcing on challenge domains are allowed)
  • DoS-ing the CTF platform or any of the challenges is forbidden. (DoS is strictly prohibited)
  • If you found any of the teams breaking the above rules, please contact the event organisers/volunteers immediately.
  • Flag deletion is strictly prohibited and defaulters will be eliminated immediately
  • By participating in the contest, you hereby agree to all rules stated above, and must be strictly followed. If any discrepancy is noted, the candidacy will be immediately removed.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is a CTF?

    CTF (Capture The Flag) is a kind of information security competition that challenges contestants to solve a variety of tasks ranging from a scavenger hunt on Wikipedia to basic programming exercises, to hacking your way into a server to steal data. In these challenges, the contestant is usually asked to find a specific piece of text that may be hidden on the server or behind a webpage. This goal is called the flag, hence the name! Like many competitions, the skill level for CTFs varies between the events. Some are targeted towards professionals with experience operating on cyber security teams. These typically offer a large cash reward and can be held at a specific physical location. Other events target the high school and college student range, sometimes offering monetary support for education to those that place highly in the competition! Find more
